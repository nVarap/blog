How do you think this happened?
There are two reasons why this could happen. Either it is as a result of white developers making and testing it solely on white people, or it was just a negligently bug as a result of poor light being picked up from the camera. I would say that it is the former, because even if there was no actual racist intent, it was very possible that those behind this were not testing it on all skin colors.
Is this harmful? Was it intended to be harmful or exclude?
I don’t feel it was harmful to exclude, but simply a byproduct of the not so diverse workforce working on this program. At the end of the day, I feel it could be harmful, because not only is this possibly a waste of money for anyone who bought it, as this man did. It may make people similarly feel excluded or cheated.

Absolutely, not only does it appear racist, it is simply fraudulent advertising. Note everybody can have their faced tracked doesn’t essentially means that they don’t exist to the computer, which is terrible and bad PR. Therefore, it absolutely should be corrected.
